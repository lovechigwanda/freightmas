SECTION 13 — SYSTEM WORKING INSTRUCTIONS
Invoicing Process in FreightMas

This section provides step-by-step guidance for each role when performing invoicing
tasks in the FreightMas system. It supplements the process flows in Section 4 with
the exact screens, buttons, and fields to use at each step.

Button labels and field names are shown in CAPITALS or described exactly as they
appear on screen.

================================================================================
13.1  SALES INVOICING STREAM
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP S1 — FORWARDING: Create and Submit an Invoicing Instruction
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Invoicing Instruction is the formal written instruction from Forwarding to the
Bookkeeper. It is a system document in its own right and is separate from the
Invoice Register Entry.

  1.  Navigate to the Invoicing workspace and click:
      Invoicing Instruction → New

  2.  Set the Company field.

  3.  In the Forwarding Job field, type the job ID, customer reference, or BL
      number to search for and link the correct job.
      The Customer and Currency fields are fetched automatically from the job.

  4.  Add the charges to be invoiced. Two options are available:

      Option A — Fetch from job (recommended):
        Click the FETCH CHARGES FROM JOB button.
        The system loads all uninvoiced revenue charges from the job into the
        line items table. Review the lines and remove any that are not yet
        ready to bill.

      Option B — Enter manually:
        In the Line Items table, add one row per charge:
          - Charge        (select the charge/item from the list)
          - Description   (auto-filled from the charge; edit if needed)
          - Qty
          - Sell Rate
          - Customer      (defaults to the job customer; change per line if billing
                           different customers on the same job)
        The Amount per line and Total Amount are calculated automatically.

  5.  Verify the Total Amount at the bottom of the form.

  6.  Add any Notes for the Bookkeeper (e.g. special payment terms, client
      purchase order number, billing instructions).

  7.  Click SUBMIT.
        - The Instruction status becomes Submitted.
        - The system automatically creates a linked Invoice Register Entry
          (Sales type) at status Instruction Received, copying all line
          items across as charge detail rows.
        - A confirmation message displays a link to the new Invoice Register
          Entry.

  NOTE: Once submitted, the Invoicing Instruction cannot be edited. If a
  correction is needed before the Bookkeeper has acted, cancel the Instruction
  (this automatically cancels the linked Invoice Register Entry if it is still
  at Instruction Received) and raise a new Instruction.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP S2 — BOOKKEEPER: Review the Invoice Register Entry and Advance to Drafted
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Invoice Register Entry (IRE) is created automatically when the Invoicing
Instruction is submitted. The Bookkeeper does not create it manually.

  1.  Find the IRE via the Invoice Register list view (filter by
      Status = Instruction Received) or follow the link shown in the
      submission confirmation message.

  2.  Open the IRE and verify the following:

      - Forwarding Job and Customer are correct.
      - Currency and Exchange Rate match the agreed billing currency.
      - Charge Details table matches the Invoicing Instruction:
          check charge item, description, qty, and sell rate on each row.
      - Tax templates are applied correctly to any taxable lines.
        (Each taxable line should have an Item Tax Template selected;
         Line Tax Amount and Line Total are calculated automatically.)
      - Total Charge Amount and Amount (inclusive of tax) at the top
        of the form are as expected.

  3.  If everything is correct, click the DRAFT INVOICE status button
      at the top of the form.
        - Status changes to Drafted.
        - The SLA clock for Forwarding review starts.

  4.  If there is a problem (wrong job, missing charges, incorrect amounts),
      contact Forwarding before proceeding. Do not advance an entry with
      zero charge lines — the system will block it.

  5.  Notify Forwarding that the draft is ready for their review (Step S4).


------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP S3 — BOOKKEEPER: Create the Sales Invoice
------------------------------------------------------------------------

This step is performed after Forwarding has reviewed and confirmed the draft
(Step S4). The Bookkeeper creates the Frappe Sales Invoice from the IRE.

  1.  Open the IRE at status Drafted.

  2.  Click CREATE SALES INVOICE.

  3.  The system performs the following automatically:
        - Adds the charge lines to the Forwarding Job's revenue charges table.
        - Creates the Frappe Sales Invoice.
        - Links the Sales Invoice back to this IRE (the invoice number appears
          in the Linked Sales Invoice field).
        - Sets the IRE status to Issued to Client — no separate status update
          is required.
        - Marks the originating Invoicing Instruction as Actioned.

  4.  The Sales Invoice name is shown in the confirmation message. Open the
      Sales Invoice to confirm all details before handing it to Forwarding
      for submission to the client.

  NOTE: Forwarding is responsible for sending the Sales Invoice to the client.
  The Bookkeeper does not send invoices to clients directly (see Section 3.5).


------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP S4 — FORWARDING: Review the Draft Invoice
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1.  Open the IRE at status Drafted. The Bookkeeper should notify Forwarding
      when the entry is ready for review.

  2.  Verify: client name, all charge lines, amounts, currency, tax treatment,
      and payment terms.

  3.  If the draft is correct:
        Inform the Bookkeeper to proceed to Step S3 (Create Sales Invoice).
        No system action is required from Forwarding at this point.

  4.  If corrections are needed:
        Click RETURN TO DRAFT.
        A comment box appears. A reason is MANDATORY — the system will not
        allow the transition without one.
        Enter a clear description of what needs to change, for example:
          "Incorrect sell rate on Ocean Freight — should be USD 950 not USD 850."
        Status changes to Returned to Draft.
        The Bookkeeper corrects the charge lines and re-advances to Drafted
        for re-review by Forwarding.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP S5 — CANCELLING A SALES INVOICE REGISTER ENTRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - To cancel from the Invoicing Instruction (before the Bookkeeper has acted):
    Cancel the Instruction document. The system automatically cancels the
    linked IRE if it is still at Instruction Received.

  - To cancel the IRE directly at any pre-issued status:
    Open the IRE and click CANCEL.
    A comment box appears. A reason is MANDATORY.
    Enter the reason for cancellation.
    Status changes to Cancelled — no further transitions are possible.

  - If a Sales Invoice has already been created (IRE status = Issued to Client),
    the IRE is locked and cannot be cancelled. A credit note must be raised in
    the accounting system with a documented justification (see Section 6.4).

13.2  PURCHASE INVOICING STREAM
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP P1 — BOOKKEEPER: Create the Invoice Register Entry
------------------------------------------------------------------------

When a supplier invoice arrives in the dedicated mailbox, the Bookkeeper logs
it in the system on the same day.

  1.  Navigate to the Invoicing workspace and click:
      Invoice Register Entry → New

  2.  Set Entry Type = Purchase.

  3.  In the Forwarding Job field, search for the correct job. You can search by
      any of the following:
        - Job ID (e.g. FWD-00023)
        - BL number / Airway Bill number
        - Customer reference number
        - Customer name
      The search results show the job ID alongside the customer reference and
      BL number, making it straightforward to match a supplier invoice to the
      correct job even when the supplier quotes only a BL number.

  4.  In the Party field (labelled Supplier on Purchase entries), select the
      supplier.

  5.  Under the Supplier Invoice Details section, enter:
        - Supplier Invoice No.    (must be unique; the system will reject a
                                   duplicate invoice number for the same supplier)
        - Supplier Invoice Date

  6.  In the Charge Details table, add one row for each line on the supplier
      invoice:
        - Charge          (select the matching charge/item from the list)
        - Description     (auto-filled; edit to match the supplier's description
                           if needed)
        - Qty             (default 1; change if the supplier invoices by quantity)
        - Buy Rate        (the unit price from the supplier invoice)
        - Line Amount     (calculated automatically as Qty × Buy Rate)
        - Item Tax Template (select if VAT or other tax applies to this line;
                             Line Tax Amount and Line Total are then calculated
                             automatically)

  7.  Upload the supplier invoice (PDF or scan) using the Attachment field.

  8.  Save the entry.
        - Status is automatically set to Received.
        - The system starts the SLA clock for same-day routing to Forwarding.

  NOTE: Do not advance the entry to the next status until all charge lines and
  the supplier invoice attachment are in place. An entry with no charge lines
  (zero total amount) cannot be progressed — the system will block it.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP P2 — BOOKKEEPER: Route for Forwarding Approval
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1.  Open the IRE at status Received.

  2.  Click SUBMIT FOR APPROVAL.
        - Status changes to Submitted for Approval.
        - The SLA clock for Forwarding approval starts (default: 24 hours).

  3.  Notify Forwarding that a supplier invoice is pending their review. Include
      the IRE reference number and supplier name in the notification.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP P3 — FORWARDING: Approve or Query the Supplier Invoice
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Forwarding has three options when reviewing a supplier invoice at status
Submitted for Approval. Select the outcome that matches the situation:

  +---------------------------+----------------------------------------------+----------------------------------------+
  | BUTTON                    | WHEN TO USE                                  | SYSTEM ACTION                          |
  +---------------------------+----------------------------------------------+----------------------------------------+
  | READY FOR CAPTURE         | Invoice matches the agreed/quoted charges     | Status → Ready for Capture.            |
  |                           | exactly. No amendments needed.               | Returned to Bookkeeper for capture.    |
  +---------------------------+----------------------------------------------+----------------------------------------+
  | RETURNED FOR CAPTURE      | Invoice is approved but the Bookkeeper        | Status → Returned for Capture.         |
  |                           | needs to note a correction (e.g. rounding,   | Use the Notes field or add a comment   |
  |                           | description update, minor adjustment).        | to instruct the Bookkeeper on the      |
  |                           |                                               | correction required.                   |
  +---------------------------+----------------------------------------------+----------------------------------------+
  | QUERY WITH SUPPLIER       | Invoice does not match agreed charges:        | Status → Query with Supplier.          |
  |                           | wrong rate, wrong quantity, unexpected        | A reason is MANDATORY.                 |
  |                           | charge, or invoiced item not recognised.      | Forwarding owns the resolution.        |
  +---------------------------+----------------------------------------------+----------------------------------------+

  NOTE — Query with Supplier:
  When this button is clicked, a comment box appears. A reason is MANDATORY —
  the system will not allow the transition without one. State clearly what the
  discrepancy is, for example:
    "Buy rate for Customs Examination is USD 150 per the quotation but invoice
     shows USD 220. Queried with supplier on [date]."


------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP P4 — FORWARDING: Resolve a Query with Supplier
------------------------------------------------------------------------

While the IRE is at Query with Supplier, Forwarding contacts the supplier
to resolve the discrepancy. The Bookkeeper does not capture the invoice
until the query is resolved.

  Once the supplier responds (issues a credit note, provides a revised invoice,
  or gives acceptable justification):

  1.  Open the IRE at status Query with Supplier.

  2.  If the charge amounts have changed (e.g. a revised rate was agreed),
      update the Charge Details lines before advancing. The Bookkeeper can
      assist with editing the charge rows if needed.

  3.  Click READY FOR CAPTURE.
        - Status changes to Ready for Capture.
        - The Bookkeeper proceeds to Step P5.

  4.  If the query cannot be resolved and the invoice is to be rejected:
        Click CANCEL (a reason is MANDATORY).
        Status changes to Cancelled — entry is locked.


------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP P5 — BOOKKEEPER: Capture the Invoice
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1.  Open the IRE at status Ready for Capture or Returned for Capture.

  2.  OPTIONAL — Update the Forwarding Job cost charges without creating
      the formal Purchase Invoice yet:
        Click COPY TO FORWARDING JOB WORKING COST.
        This copies the charge rows to the Forwarding Job's cost charges
        table, making the actual cost and the variance against planned
        margin immediately visible on the job to Forwarding.
        Use this option if you are updating job visibility as part of a
        batch run and will raise the formal Purchase Invoice separately.

  3.  To create the Purchase Invoice:
        Click CREATE PURCHASE INVOICE.

        The system performs the following automatically:
          - Copies charge rows to the Forwarding Job's cost charges table
            (if not already copied in step 2 above).
          - Creates the Frappe Purchase Invoice.
          - Links the Purchase Invoice back to this IRE (the invoice number
            appears in the Linked Purchase Invoice field).
          - Sets IRE status to Captured — the entry is now locked.
          - Copies the IRE attachment to the Forwarding Job for reference.

  4.  Open the Purchase Invoice to confirm all details are correct before
      saving and submitting it in the accounting system.

  NOTE — After Captured:
  The IRE is fully locked. No further edits are possible to the entry, its
  charge lines, party, currency, or amounts. Any correction requires a credit
  note in the accounting system with a documented justification (Section 6.4).


------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP P6 — CANCELLING A PURCHASE INVOICE REGISTER ENTRY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - At Received or Submitted for Approval:
    Click CANCEL and provide a mandatory reason.
    Use this when an invoice was logged in error (e.g. duplicate entry,
    wrong job linked).

  - At Query with Supplier:
    Forwarding clicks CANCEL if the invoice is rejected entirely
    (mandatory reason required).

  - At Captured or when a Purchase Invoice is linked:
    The IRE is locked and cannot be cancelled.
    Any correction must be processed as a credit note.

13.4  SLA WINDOWS AND OVERDUE ALERTS
================================================================================

The system automatically tracks how long each entry has been in its current
status. When an entry exceeds its configured SLA window, it is flagged as
OVERDUE and appears on the Overdue dashboard card in the Invoicing workspace.

  +--------------------------------+-----------+------------------+-----------------------------------+
  | STATUS                         | SLA OWNER | DEFAULT WINDOW   | DESCRIPTION                       |
  +--------------------------------+-----------+------------------+-----------------------------------+
  | Received                       | Bookkeeper| 24 hours         | Route to Forwarding for approval  |
  | Submitted for Approval         | Forwarding| 24 hours         | Approve or raise a query          |
  | Query with Supplier            | Forwarding| 2 working days   | Resolve discrepancy with supplier |
  | Instruction Received           | Bookkeeper| 3 hours          | Advance to Drafted                |
  +--------------------------------+-----------+------------------+-----------------------------------+

  - Entries at terminal states (Captured, Issued to Client, Cancelled) are
    excluded from SLA tracking.
  - The overdue flag is updated automatically by a nightly system task —
    no manual action is needed to flag or clear it.
  - SLA windows are configured in FreightMas Settings and can be adjusted by
    the Finance Manager without a system change.
  - The Invoicing workspace dashboard shows live counts:
      Open Entries, Needs Action, Overdue, and queue views by type.


================================================================================
13.5  AUDIT TRAIL
================================================================================

Every status change on an Invoice Register Entry is permanently recorded in
the Status Log tab at the bottom of the IRE form.

Each log row shows:

  - From / To status
  - Changed by       (user name)
  - Changed at       (date and time)
  - Working days in previous status  (Monday to Friday only)
  - Comment          (mandatory for Query with Supplier, Returned to Draft,
                      and Cancelled; optional for other transitions)

The log cannot be edited or deleted. It provides a complete, timestamped
record of every action taken on the entry, supporting both internal review
and external audit requirements.


================================================================================
13.6  INVOICE REGISTER ENTRY LOCKING
================================================================================

An Invoice Register Entry is locked once any of the following conditions are met:

  - Status = Captured
  - Status = Issued to Client
  - A Purchase Invoice or Sales Invoice has been linked to the entry

When locked, the following fields cannot be edited:

  Entry-level:   Company, Entry Type, Entry Date, Forwarding Job, Party,
                 Currency, Exchange Rate, all amounts, Supplier Invoice No.,
                 Supplier Invoice Date, Attachment, linked invoice references.

  Charge rows:   Charge, Description, Qty, Rate, Tax Template, all calculated
                 amounts.

A locked entry also cannot be deleted.

If a correction is needed after an entry is locked, raise a credit note in
the accounting system with a documented justification, per Section 6.4 of
this SOP.
